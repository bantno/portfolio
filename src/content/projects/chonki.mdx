## The Challenge

Design and build a competitive 45 lb combat robot within a collegiate team, iterating across multiple competition seasons at NHRL — the premier US combat robotics league.

## Approach

Designed and fabricated a 45 lb featherweight combat robot through iterative design cycles spanning weapon systems, armor, and drivetrain optimization. Held key leadership roles over 6 years in Georgia Tech's RoboJackets: Mechanical Training Lead (2019–2020), 3 lb Mentor (2019–2020), Sponsorship Chair (2020–2021), Team Lead (2020–2021), and Team Mentor (2021–Present).

Each competition season drove redesign: weapon geometry changes to improve bite and energy storage, armor material and thickness tradeoffs to survive increasingly powerful opponents, and drivetrain gearing adjustments for the right balance of speed and pushing power.

## Iteration

Multi-season design evolution drove Chonki from a competitive entry to a dominant force. Early iterations prioritized weapon energy at the expense of reliability — a lesson learned through catastrophic gearbox failures in early matches. Subsequent versions shifted to a more balanced energy budget: slightly lower weapon tip speed with dramatically improved drivetrain durability.

The armor strategy evolved from uniform AR500 panels to a hybrid approach with hardened steel in high-impact zones and lighter aluminum in areas protected by the weapon's reach. This saved 3 lbs reallocated to the drivetrain.

Beyond Chonki, designed and built a shipping-container testing arena — the BattleBox — with high-speed cameras and internal armor, funded by the Georgia Tech Student Foundation, planned to be shared with local universities for combat robotics development.
