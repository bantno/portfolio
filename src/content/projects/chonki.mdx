Combat robotics pits custom-built machines against each other in an enclosed arena — Chonki competes in the featherweight class (30 lb, or 45 lb with the shuffler weight bonus) at NHRL, the premier US combat robotics league.

## The Challenge

Design and build a competitive combat robot within a collegiate team, iterating across multiple competition seasons.

## Approach

Designed and fabricated a 45 lb featherweight combat robot through iterative design cycles spanning weapon systems, armor, and drivetrain optimization. Held key leadership roles over 6 years in Georgia Tech's RoboJackets: Mechanical Training Lead (2019–2020), 3 lb Mentor (2019–2020), Sponsorship Chair (2020–2021), Team Lead (2020–2021), and Team Mentor (2021–Present).

Each competition season drove redesign: weapon geometry changes to improve bite and energy storage, armor material and thickness tradeoffs to survive increasingly powerful opponents, and drivetrain gearing adjustments for the right balance of speed and pushing power.

## Iteration

Chonki started as a name, not a design. The practical goal: exploit the 1.5x weight bonus that NHRL grants robots using non-wheeled locomotion, and use that extra mass to evaluate whether shell spinners could be competitive in the featherweight class.

### Chonki (2020)

The original Chonki was designed during the COVID-19 pandemic but never built — manufacturing restrictions made fabrication impossible. It existed only in CAD, but established the shell spinner + shuffler drivetrain architecture that all future versions would build on.

### Chonkii (2021–2022)

The first physical Chonki. Big, loud, and impossible to ignore — the shufflers scraping on steel arena floors required hearing protection at competition. Chonkii proved the concept was viable, beating Marathon (ranked #3 at the time) at Motorama, but had significant room for improvement.

### Chonkiii (2022–2023)

A major design leap. Chonkiii introduced a hardened 4140 alloy steel shell — proving the CNC billet approach that would define all future versions. It added a sloped wall shell design, reduced ground clearance, a self-righter, and dual weapon drive motors. Chonkiii was the first Chonki to compete at NHRL and was more competitive, but still had a ways to go: two-piece impactors were prone to bolt failures, the 2:1 drive gearing was underpowered, and the shuffler modules — while reliable — lacked the torque to drive effectively against strong opponents.

### Chonkiv (2023–2025)

Chonkiv was a systematic redesign targeting every Chonkiii shortcoming:

- **Teeth:** Single-piece sharpened AR600 toothicanes replaced the failure-prone two-piece AR500 impactors, with a larger rake angle for better bite depth and cutting efficiency.
- **Drive:** 3:1 bevel gear reduction (up from 2:1) delivered substantially more pushing power — enough to push opponents without the shell spinning.
- **Shuffler pods:** Modular, self-contained units that can be field-swapped in ~15 minutes for rapid pit repairs.
- **Electronics:** 3D-printed enclosures with dedicated battery slots and wire-routing channels replaced the previous loose wiring.
- **Frame:** Puzzle-fit 7075 aluminum with M4 fasteners (up from M3), waterjet-cut to tight tolerances for a rigid chassis with minimal fastener reliance.
