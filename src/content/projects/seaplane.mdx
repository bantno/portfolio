## Mission & Energy Planning

A solar-powered UAV must decide when to fly, when to rest on the water and recharge, and how to route itself given uncertain weather and energy constraints. Simple threshold rules — fly when battery is above some level, land when it drops below another — are intuitive but suboptimal.

I developed a Markov Decision Process framework solved via dynamic programming that governs flight planning for long-duration missions, balancing energy constraints and environmental risks. This improved mission survivability by 50% over baseline threshold algorithms.

The vehicle must decide when to fly and when to rest on the water to recharge. Can you design a rule that outperforms the optimal policy?

The key insight: the optimal policy makes non-obvious decisions — like resting during a sunny period to build energy reserves for a risky overnight segment — that no simple threshold can replicate.

## Vehicle Design & Analysis

The seaplane's design integrates aerodynamic performance, hydrostatic stability, and solar energy harvesting into a single airframe.

**Aerodynamic analysis** in VSPAero estimated drag polars, lift coefficients, and stability derivatives using finite difference methods. **Hydrostatic stability analysis** calculated transverse and longitudinal metacentric heights to ensure roll stability while floating. **Solar integration** uses Ascent Solar CIGS thin-film modules with an MPPT controller and Battery Management System for autonomous recharging. A custom **spanwise wing rotation system** enables solar tracking to maximize energy capture.

## Build, Integrate & Test

Constructed a prototype amphibious UAV at 3.0 kg. Fabrication included composites manufacturing and custom mechanism machining. Integrated solar modules, MPPT controller, BMS, and PX4 flight controller into the airframe.

Conducted maiden flight testing: validated takeoff run of 100 ft and glide ratio of 6.4. Used flight logs to tune PX4 control parameters. Led a flight test program executing sorties that validated theoretical power models — 150W cruise at 18 m/s — and diagnosed critical hydrodynamic instabilities driving rapid design iterations.

<section id="team-iteration">

## Team & Iteration

Led a team of 8 undergraduate researchers, guiding them across machine design, composites manufacturing, controls, and flight testing.

Diagnosed critical hydrodynamic instabilities from flight test data, driving rapid hull redesign. The original hull cross-section produced unacceptable spray patterns during water taxi, causing wing wetting and loss of solar panel efficiency. Iterative testing with modified hull geometries resolved the instability within two design cycles.

Iterative control parameter tuning from flight log analysis refined the PX4 gains for the seaplane's unique flight envelope — low wing loading and high aspect ratio demanded significantly different tuning from standard quadcopter or fixed-wing defaults.

**Published:** B. Epstein and J. D. Rogers, "Conceptual Design of a Low-Altitude Solar Powered Seaplane for Oceanic Monitoring," AIAA AVIATION FORUM AND ASCEND 2024.
